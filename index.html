Introducing Bella Week

I'm a student at CUNY's Graduate School of Journalism, hoping to become an investigative reporter. I want to produce work that holds powerful systems accountable and redistributes power to communities through knowledge and access to information.

Here are three pieces of journalism I admire:

  1. The Shadow Penal System for Struggling Kids, by Rachel Aviv – The New Yorker.

    2. This story exposes “troubled teen” programs that abuse and neglect the kids they claim to help. I appreciate how Aviv centers the kids’ experiences with respect and empathy, and seeks to hold harmful institutions accountable.

  2. It’s Bisan From Gaza, I’m Still Alive, by Bisan Owda – AJ+.

    3. Owda reports from Gaza under extremely dangerous conditions, documenting a genocide by a military that has killed hundreds of journalists and restricted independent coverage. Her reporting is courageous, essential, and far-reaching.

  3. The Giant Pool of Money, by Alex Blumberg & Adam Davidson – This American Life.

    4. I first listened to this story at seventeen, when I had little interest in or understanding of the economy. It helped me make sense of the 2008 financial crisis, the people behind it, and the decisions that caused it. I like how it uses storytelling to explain complex financial systems while keeping nuance intact.
